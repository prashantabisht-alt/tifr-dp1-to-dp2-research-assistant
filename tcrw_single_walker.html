TCRW single-walker trajectory

Topological Chiral Random Walk (Osat–Speck style): chirality lives in rotation, not translation. Walker translates deterministically forward in its current director at rate 1. Rotation event at rate Dr; given rotation, CW with probability ω, CCW with probability 1 − ω. ω = 0.5 ⇒ achiral. ω → 0 or 1 ⇒ maximal chirality.

How to read it: Dr controls how often rotation happens; ω controls whether rotation prefers one chirality. At ω = 0.5 the walker random-walks its director (no net circular tendency). At ω close to 0 or 1 the walker tends to turn in one direction consistently, producing spiral / circulating trajectories. Compare with JMVR: this same chirality-from-rotation is conceptually what makes TCRW topologically non-trivial on the square lattice — and the open research question is whether that topology survives the 4-fold → 6-fold change.
