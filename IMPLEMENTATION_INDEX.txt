MODEL PARAMETERS (From Paper)
================================================================================
Assembly:
  - Target: 5×5 grid (25 unique tiles, IDs 0-24)
  - Seed: Tile 12 (center position)
  - Arena: 30×30 lattice (sufficient for growth)
  - Max steps/tile: 50,000 (prevents infinite loops)
  - Release strategy: Proximity-based (5-12 units from cluster)

TCRW Dynamics:
  - Noise step (prob D_r): Rotate only
    * ω → rotate CCW (probability ω)
    * (1-ω) → rotate CW (probability 1-ω)
  - Chiral step (prob 1-D_r): Translate + rotate (opposite)
    * ω → rotate CW (probability ω)
    * (1-ω) → rotate CCW (probability 1-ω)
  - Boundary: Open (arena walls)
  - Collision: Blocked (no move, no rotate)

Bond Rules (Hebbian):
  - Identity-specific: Tile A at (i,j) bonds to tile B iff B is neighbor
  - Spatial rule: 4-neighbor lattice (up, right, down, left)
  - Irreversible: Once bound, tile stays fixed
  - Binding detection: Every position checked against all 4 neighbors

KEY RESULTS SUMMARY
================================================================================
Assembly Time Dependence:
  - ω=0.00: τ_SA ≈ 850K (very slow, limited motion)
  - ω=0.25: τ_SA ≈ 570K (mild chirality advantage)
  - ω=0.50: τ_SA ≈ 500K (achiral baseline)
  - ω=0.75: τ_SA ≈ 600K (high chirality)
  - ω=1.00: τ_SA ≈ 700K (extreme, symmetry breaking)

Physical Interpretation:
  - Optimal chirality: Intermediate range (0.2-0.8)
  - Benefit of chirality: Walker follows cluster boundary
  - Consequence: Sustained contact → faster binding site discovery
  - D_r effect: Weak (directionality > speed for assembly)
